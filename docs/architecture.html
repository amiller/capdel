architecture

One idea, three layers: capability delegation.

capdel, oauth3, and dstack-webhost are the same move applied at different depths — hand out a narrow, revocable slice of authority instead of the raw thing, and keep enforcement on your side. Each delegates something different: attested compute, your web accounts, and your local resources.

the three, by what they delegate

Same primitive, three domains.

dstack-webhostthe ground

The attested TEE app host — tee-daemon running inside a dstack (Phala) confidential VM. You hand it a tarball + manifest and it runs your code source-bound and measurable, gives it a URL, injects config via ctx.env, and hands out persistent storage via ctx.dataDir.

delegates attested computeenforces measurement · isolation
oauth3account layer

An app running on dstack-webhost. It holds sealed cookie jars keyed by subject:plugin, mints scoped revocable tokens, and runs the connect → approve consent flow. Its lane: "let this app act as me on a site I have a session to, without my password."

delegates your web accountsenforces who gets which jar-scope
capdelresource layer

A local reference monitor. It delegates fs · exec · net · llm between agents of different trust — minted at dispatch time, attenuated, escalatable, revocable to the leaf. Runs on your machine; a dstack pod can act as a dial-out relay so a remote agent reaches it without the authority ever leaving home.

delegates local / agent resourcesenforces what a holder may do
they compose

teleport-pod uses all three at once.

The post-once voucher app is the whole stack in one place — it never holds a raw secret, only borrowed capabilities.

  1. Runs on dstack-webhost. Deployed as a pod app; reads its config from ctx.env, persists vouchers in ctx.dataDir, served at /teleport-pod — attested like any project.
  2. Borrows an account through oauth3. A visitor clicks connect → approves on the oauth3 screen → the pod gets a jar-cap token and posts as them, without their X password ever touching the app.
  3. Borrows a key through capdel. Its content filter calls the shared model via a capdel llm capability — the broker injects the key, the pod holds only a revocable cap token. Kill it and the app is cut off instantly.
